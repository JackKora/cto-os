# Managing Sideways

**Scope:** Lateral relationships with peers — fellow leaders across functions (CPO, VP Sales, VP Marketing, Head of Ops, Legal, Finance, etc.) where the user has to get things done without reporting authority. Tracks peer profiles (observable behavior + what currencies they value), 1:1 prep and capture, negotiation preparation for substantial cross-functional negotiations, and the running state of coalitions and cross-functional initiatives.

**Out of scope:** Direct reports (Managing Down); upward relationships (Managing Up); external peer CTOs and industry peers (External Network & Thought Leadership); board-level stakeholders (Board Comms); large-audience internal comms (Org Comms).

**Frameworks:** [Allan Cohen & David Bradford — *Influence Without Authority*](https://www.amazon.com/Influence-Without-Authority-Allan-Cohen/dp/0471463302), [Roger Fisher & William Ury — *Getting to Yes*](https://www.amazon.com/Getting-Yes-Negotiating-Agreement-Without/dp/0143118757).

**Depends on:**
- Required: none
- Optional: `personal-os` (voice, show-up), `business-alignment` (shared company-level goals when negotiating), `attention-operations` (surfaces peer-originated items)

**Example tasks:**
- "Prep for my 1:1 with the CPO tomorrow."
- "Log my 1:1 with the VP of Sales — what came out of it."
- "Prep for the roadmap negotiation with the CPO on Friday."
- "The VP of Marketing keeps deprioritizing the ask — help me reframe."
- "Build the coalition for the SOC 2 push across eng, sales, and legal."
