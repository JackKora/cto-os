# Org Design

**Scope:** Optimizing how engineering is organized to deliver value. Authors reorg proposals, logs structural decisions, analyzes bottlenecks through Reinertsen + Conway's Law, assembles current-state org snapshots. Low-frequency, high-leverage — most of the time no active proposals are in flight. Strategic and periodic module.

**Out of scope:** Day-to-day team health tracking (Team Management); hiring pipeline execution (Hiring); individual performance (Performance & Development); flow measurement inside existing structures (Process Management — this module consumes its output).

**Frameworks:** [Matthew Skelton & Manuel Pais — *Team Topologies*](https://teamtopologies.com/), [Conway's Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conway%27s_law), value-flow lens via [Reinertsen's *Flow*](https://www.amazon.com/Principles-Product-Development-Flow-Generation/dp/1935401009) (consumed through Process Management).

**Depends on:**
- Required: `process-management`, `business-alignment`
- Optional: `team-management` (current team state), `hiring` (feasibility of proposed moves), `budget` (cost of change)

**Example tasks:**
- "Draft a reorg proposal: merge platform and infra-ops into a single team."
- "Diagnose the integration-layer bottleneck — is it Conway or Reinertsen?"
- "Log the decision: we're going stream-aligned across product teams."
- "Show me the current org snapshot."
- "Update design principles — we're adding 'every team has a clear primary user.'"
